Divergent Global Agendas: A Textual Analysis of UNGA 80th Session Speeches

Summary: Quantitative computational text analysis of five UNGA 80th session transcripts (Brazil, China, France, Nigeria, USA). The study utilises sentiment analysis, Topic Modeling (LDA), and keyword frequency to reveal a sharp ideological split in global discourse. Findings show that four nations overwhelmingly focused on multilateral cooperation, development, and climate action, evidenced by high positive sentiment (Nigeria highest, around 120) and convergence on shared topics. The USA, conversely, articulated a highly critical, nationalist agenda (only negative sentiment score, around -40), centered on border sovereignty, critique of Europe's energy policies, and rejection of climate agreements (calling it a "hoax").

Stack: R, LaTeX, tidytext, topicmodels, LDA, Sentiment Analysis, Word Count.

The Digital Silk Road in Europe: A Narrative Mapping of Chinese Infrastructure

Summary: A spatial-narrative study of Chinese digital influence within Europe, mapping critical nodes of friction between EU security policy and economic pragmatism. This interactive analysis tracks four high-impact cases (the PEACE cable in France, AI governance hubs in Serbia, logistics centres in Hungary, and police surveillance infrastructure in Spain) to reveal how "technological alignment" creates path dependency and complicates Western intelligence alliances.

Stack: StoryMapJS, Geopolitical Risk Analysis, Spatial Research.
